## 12. SUMMARY & STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS

### The Good News
- The site already has a Mobile Surveillance Trailer page with decent content
- Strong commercial credibility through client logos (FGCU, Dunkin', government entities)
- Owner has 30+ years in the industry
- WordPress platform is flexible for restructuring
- Domain has age and authority (since ~2018 in current form, business since 2001)
- Florida licensing already in place
- Google Reviews integration provides ongoing social proof

### The Bad News
- Site is overwhelmingly residential in tone and content
- Mobile Surveillance Trailer page has zero photos, no specs, no pricing, no case studies
- Blog completely dormant for 5+ years
- No modern lead capture (no chat, no scheduling, no resources)
- No industry-specific landing pages
- Missing critical commercial content (case studies, ROI data, deployment info)
- Two different license numbers shown (inconsistency)
- Videos page is empty
- Local landing pages are duplicate content
- No email listed publicly
- No social media presence visible

### Bottom Line
The bones are there — the site has domain authority, a WordPress foundation, existing commercial pages, and the Mobile Surveillance Trailer page. But the current site is **80% residential, 20% commercial** and needs to flip to **20% residential, 80% commercial/mobile surveillance** to support the pivot. The most critical gap is real-world proof: photos of actual deployed trailers, case studies, and deployment specs. Without those, the Mobile Surveillance Trailer page reads like a generic article, not a sales page.
